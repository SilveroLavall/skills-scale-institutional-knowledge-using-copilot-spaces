# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own the user experience and usability of features, ensuring that solutions are intuitive, accessible, and meet user needs. They collaborate closely with Product Managers and Developers to translate requirements into user-centered designs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Define and validate usability acceptance criteria
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation feasibility
- Iterate on designs based on feedback and metrics
- Ensure accessibility and design consistency

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce support burden through better UX
- Maximize user adoption and satisfaction

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on user research findings and acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Reviews implementation against design specifications; provides feedback on technical constraints
- **Project Manager**: Updates timeline if usability issues require design iteration
- **Data Analyst**: Reviews user engagement metrics to inform design improvements

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and user feedback sessions
- Usability testing reports and design documentation
- Participation in planning to review design scope and estimates

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts track, measure, and report on project success metrics and product performance. They work with Product Managers and Project Managers to establish measurement plans and provide data-driven insights to inform prioritization and iteration.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track success metrics and KPIs
- Set up dashboards and reporting infrastructure
- Analyze project and product performance data
- Identify trends, anomalies, and improvement opportunities
- Support A/B testing and experimentation
- Generate insights and recommendations for the team

### Goals
- Provide clear visibility into project and product health
- Enable data-driven decision-making
- Demonstrate measurable impact of delivered features

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on success metrics definition; provides insights for prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Reports on project health metrics and progress toward goals
- **Developers**: Works with team to instrument code and track technical metrics
- **UX Designer**: Analyzes user engagement and usability metrics

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metric reviews and dashboard updates
- Data-driven insights in stakeholder briefings
- Ad-hoc analysis for decision support

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Lead

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters or Delivery Leads facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and ensure the team stays aligned and focused on sprint commitments. They act as a servant leader, supporting the team's capacity to deliver consistently.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, and retrospectives
- Identify and help resolve blockers and dependencies
- Track sprint velocity and iteration health
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Escalate issues that require management attention
- Ensure the team follows Definition of Done

### Goals
- Enable consistent, predictable delivery
- Foster psychological safety and team autonomy
- Continuously improve team processes and velocity

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates escalations and resource constraints
- **Developers**: Removes blockers; facilitates technical discussions
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog readiness; clarifies acceptance criteria
- **All Roles**: Advocates for team capacity and sustainable pace

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of sprint ceremonies
- Daily blocker identification and resolution
- Retrospective action item tracking
- Escalation of dependencies and risks

---

## Customer Success / Support Liaison

### Role Summary
Customer Success and Support Liaisons serve as the bridge between field teams (support, sales, customer success) and the delivery team. They channel customer feedback, issues, and requests back to the product and engineering teams, ensuring customer voice informs prioritization and issue resolution.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and prioritize customer feedback and feature requests
- Report on customer-facing issues and their impact
- Escalate critical production issues and customer blockers
- Track customer satisfaction and support metrics
- Participate in sprint planning to advocate for customer needs
- Communicate feature releases and updates back to support teams

### Goals
- Reduce customer churn through responsive issue resolution
- Increase customer satisfaction and advocacy
- Ensure product roadmap reflects customer needs

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Channels customer insights into roadmap prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Escalates urgent customer issues; reports on customer impact
- **Developers**: Provides context on customer impact; validates fixes
- **Data Analyst**: Analyzes support metrics and trends

### Typical Communication
- Weekly customer issue triage and prioritization
- Stakeholder updates on customer impact and sentiment
- Participation in planning to review customer-impacting backlog
- Release notes and communication to support teams

- When planning a project, ensure representation from relevant personas and clarify their responsibilities and interdependencies.
- Use the "Interaction with Other Roles" section to identify communication touchpoints and dependencies.
